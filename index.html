About GrowShare

Our Mission

GrowShare Cincinnati is dedicated to addressing food insecurity in our city by providing families with the tools and knowledge to grow their own fresh produce at home, regardless of space limitations or climate conditions.

Over 23% of residents in some Cincinnati neighborhoods experience limited access to fresh produce, according to the Freestore Foodbank (2024). Our grow tent packages enable year-round cultivation of nutritious vegetables, promoting self-sufficiency and encouraging community sharing of surplus harvests.

Our Vision

We envision a Cincinnati where everyone has access to fresh, healthy food, where neighbors support each other through shared resources, and where sustainable growing practices become a standard part of everyday life—starting with our focus neighborhoods of Avondale, Price Hill, and Westwood.

Project Proposal

Executive Summary

GrowShare seeks a $10,000 grant to launch a pilot program providing indoor grow tent systems to 5 families experiencing food insecurity in Cincinnati, Ohio. This initiative aims to create sustainable food sources for these families while establishing a model for community sharing and potential expansion. We'll prioritize families in Avondale, Price Hill, and Westwood neighborhoods, where food insecurity rates are highest. Additionally, we'll develop a content generation service focusing on urban agriculture to create supplemental revenue for program sustainability.

Organization Structure

Recommended Structure: 501(c)(3) Non-Profit Organization

We recommend establishing GrowShare as a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization rather than an LLC for the following reasons:

  • Tax-exempt status allows all funds to directly support program operations
  • Eligibility for a wider range of grants and philanthropic funding
  • Enhanced credibility with government agencies, donors, and beneficiaries
  • Ability to offer tax deductions to donors and sponsors
  • Mission-focused governance structure that aligns with the community benefit purpose

While the content generation service component will generate revenue, as a non-profit, these funds will be reinvested directly into expanding the grow tent program.

Program Details

Grow Tent Initiative:

  • Target Population: 5 families experiencing food insecurity in our initial phase
  • Selection Criteria: Food insecurity status, housing stability, commitment to program participation, willingness to share surplus with community
  • Equipment: Complete indoor grow systems including tent, LED lighting, climate control, hydroponic setup, nutrients, and initial seed stock
  • Support: Training sessions, ongoing technical assistance, and community connection facilitation
  • Community Sharing: Facilitated system for participants to share surplus with neighbors in need

Content Generation Service:

  • Service Offering: Custom content creation for sustainable farming, gardening, and food-related businesses
  • Target Clients: Garden supply companies, seed providers, nutrition organizations, and community farms
  • Content Types: Blog posts, social media content, email newsletters, educational materials
  • Pricing Model: Subscription-based packages with different tiers based on content volume and complexity
  • Revenue Use: 100% of profits directed to expanding the grow tent program

Expected Outcomes & Impact Measurement

Quantitative Metrics:

  • 5 active grow systems producing greens and herbs in Cincinnati homes
  • Estimated 150–200 lbs of produce grown across 6 months
  • $150–300 average monthly savings in food cost per family
  • 20–30% of produce shared with community partners
  • Minimum 3 paying content creation clients secured in year 1

Qualitative Metrics:

  • Increased self-efficacy in food growing (survey-based)
  • Improved nutritional awareness
  • Strengthened local Cincinnati community networks
  • Positive media engagement and brand visibility

Content Generation Service (Social Enterprise Arm)

To ensure long-term sustainability, GrowShare Cincinnati will launch a specialized content creation service focusing on urban agriculture and sustainability media:

  • Service Offerings: SEO blog articles, educational videos, short-form content for brands in agriculture, food, and sustainability
  • Target Clients: Local gardening suppliers (e.g., Daisy Jane's Flower Farm, Turner Farm), national seed and nutrient brands, and nonprofits seeking storytelling on food justice work
  • Revenue Model: Tiered monthly subscription packages with 100% of revenue reinvested into expanding the Grow Tent program
  • Year 1 Goal: Secure minimum 3 paying content creation clients
  • Year 2 Goal: Fund 3 additional grow tents using revenue from content clients and sponsorships without additional grant dependency

Sustainability Plan

GrowShare is designed for long-term sustainability through a hybrid funding model:

  1. Content Generation Service: As our primary revenue stream, this service will grow to fund additional grow tent systems beyond the initial 5 families
  2. Ongoing Grants: With demonstrated impact from our pilot, we'll apply for larger government and foundation grants
  3. Sponsorships: Corporate sponsorships from agricultural, food, and sustainability-focused businesses
  4. Individual Donations: Community-based support through recurring donation programs
  5. Equipment Partnerships: Negotiating discounted or donated equipment from manufacturers aligned with our mission

By year 2, we project the content service will generate sufficient revenue to fund 2-3 additional grow tent systems annually without requiring additional grant funding.
